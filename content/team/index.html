Our Team

We are Engineers. Physicians. Designers. Scientists. Together, we're working to bring mobile health technology to the underserved.

Product Development

The product team leverage their expertise in the fields of software development, design, and public health to create our technology platform and disease self-management programs.

Deepak Kothule

Vice President of Engineering

Deepak has over 20+ years of software engineering experience, including 10+ years of engineering management. Most recently, Deepak served as the Vice President of Engineering at TigerText, a HIPAA-compliant secure messaging application for hospitals. Before TigerText, Deepak led engineering management teams at Symantec (NASDAQ:SYMC) and Salesforce (NYSE:CRM), where he lead the development of products like Norton Internet Security and Salesforce Mobile, Chatter. Deepak holds an MBA in Technology Management and Bachelors in Computer Science.

Cecilia Corral

Vice President of Product
Co-Founder

Cecilia oversees all aspects of product development, including the core CareMessage application and associated disease management content. She works with partner organizations and patients to design, test and refine CareMessage products. She is proficient in the process of human-centered design pioneered by the Hasso Plattner Institute of Design (d.school) at Stanford University, where she graduated with a degree in Product Design Engineering. A native Spanish speaker who grew up in South Texas and Mexico, Cecilia helps ensure that all CareMessage programs accommodate the cultural and linguistic needs of our minority patients.

Daniel Naves

Engineering Manager

He has previously built multiple web and mobile platforms for both startups and Fortune 500 companies, and loves working on important projects that can have a significant impact.

Patricia Sanchez, MPH

Director of Content

Patricia helps develop many of CareMessage's programs, especially those focused on childhood and adolescence health issues. She comes to the organization with a passion to improve health literacy among low income populations. She has worked with a Medi-Cal managed care population at the Contra Costa Health Plan, and holds a Masters in Public Health (MPH) from San Diego State University.

Nicco Turner, MA

Health Content Specialist, Chronic Disease Management

Nicco helps to write and customize culturally relevant messages for Care Message’s users based on cultural relevance, community needs, and trends at an appropriate literacy level. She comes to the organization with a passion for health and wellness. She has over 10 years of experience in social services and health education working with diverse populations. She hopes to empower and motivate people to make positive health changes that will change their lives. She has worked as a health coach and a natural chef. Greg Burrell, MD

Director of Research and Clinical Solutions

Greg leads the clinical review and oversight of CareMessage's programs and works with the Scientific Advisory Board to produce high-quality data to help drive informed decisions about CareMessage's clinical products. He comes to the organization with a passion to educate and empower patients to make smarter healthcare decisions, as well as creating and implementing new technologies to improve healthcare outcomes. Greg is currently an Assistant Professor of Clinical Medicine at the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF). He received his bachelors degree from Northwestern, his medical degree from the Pritzker School of Medicine at the University of Chicago, and completed his internal medicine residency at UCSF.

Nina Parikh, MAT, MPH

Research Associate

Nina helps support the design, development and implementation of research projects at CareMessage. Before joining CareMessage, she worked at the Stanford Prevention Research Center where she helped perform research on the retail availability and marketing of tobacco products in underserved communities.  She also worked as a high school biology teacher and program director of a college-prep program that served under-performing students in low-income high schools.  Nina is passionate about working to alleviate health disparities and earned a master’s degree in teaching from National Louis University and a master’s degree in public health from Washington University in St. Louis.
